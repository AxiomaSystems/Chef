# ElevenLabs Voiceover Script - Butter Me Core App Demo

Last updated: May 25, 2026

Use this document to generate staged voiceover clips in ElevenLabs for a product demo, campus ad, or social video. The script is intentionally divided into short sections so each stage can be recorded separately and synced to app screen recordings.

## Voice Direction

- Voice style: warm, clear, confident, student-friendly.
- Pace: medium-fast, but not rushed.
- Tone: practical and energetic, not corporate.
- Target audience: students and young adults who want to cook but do not want to plan groceries manually.
- Pronunciation:
  - Butter Me: say it like "butter me."
  - Hands-free: clear pause between "hands" and "free."
  - Shopping cart: say as two words, not "ShoppingCART."

Suggested ElevenLabs settings:

- Stability: 45-60%.
- Similarity: 75-85%.
- Style/exaggeration: low to medium.
- Speaker boost: on if available.

## Core Message

Butter Me helps users go from:

```text
What should I cook?
-> What do I already have?
-> What recipe do I want?
-> What am I missing?
-> Buy it.
-> Cook hands-free.
```

The three core features to keep repeating:

1. Recipe selection and recipe addition.
2. One-tap shopping for ingredients the user does not already have.
3. Agent-guided, voice-supported hands-free cooking.

## Notes For Video Editing

- Keep each line synced to visible button taps.
- Show circles around the exact buttons users should tap.
- Do not say "checkout is complete inside Butter Me" unless the actual provider flow supports that claim.
- Do not say the app replaces food safety judgment, allergy advice, or medical nutrition advice.
- For campus ads, include a QR code, campus code, or landing page so signups can be attributed.
- Best first CTA: "Try your first recipe" instead of "Subscribe now."
